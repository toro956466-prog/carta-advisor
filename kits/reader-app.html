The long end is finally paying you to wait.

Tyler Ontiveros
Published 7:15 AM ET · Updated 9:42 AM

TLT closed −0.82% yesterday after the 10‑year re‑tested 4.62%, and the long end of the curve is doing something it hasn't done since early 2024 — paying you to wait. That's the trade today.

We're adding duration. 2% of book, sized into weakness, with a hard stop below the March pivot at 88.90. This is a duration trade, not a call on the Fed. The distinction matters.

Here's the thesis in four sentences. Real yields on the long end cleared 2.2% last week — the highest positive real carry on 20‑year paper since 2007. Growth is cooling at the edges (ISM services softened, continuing claims grinding up), but the Fed won't cut until it sees two more CPI prints under 2.5%. That's a window where duration gets paid, not punished. Size accordingly.

The trade is duration exposure, not a bet on the Fed.

The risk I'm watching

Supply. Treasury refunding lands May 4, and if the quarterly issuance tilts further toward the long end, the curve steepens on us and TLT re‑tests the March low. That's why the stop sits where it does — below the pivot, not at the pivot. If we break 88.90 with conviction, the thesis is wrong and we're out for −2%.

Second risk: oil. A move to $92 WTI resets the inflation picture in a week and the Fed speak shifts hawkish. Watch USO and the 6‑month calendar spread — the market tells you before the data does.
